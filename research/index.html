Areas

Pushing the boundaries of how machines perceive and interact with the world.

Advanced Visual Perception

This project focuses on scene-understanding computer vision tasks including object detection, segmentation (instance, semantic, and panoptic), and depth estimation or completion from image, video, and point-cloud sequences using supervised, semi-supervised, few-shot, and self-supervised learning techniques.

Basic Vision Tasks Multi-Object Tracking Human Face, Emotion, Action, and Social Group & Activity Detection 3D Reconstruction and Mapping A Dataset and Multi-task 3D Visual Perception System for a Mobile Robot in Human Environments Visual Reasoning

Forecasting & Behavior

Forecasting human trajectory and body motion is positioned on the legacy site as a core capability for robotics, healthcare, and surveillance. The emphasis is on physically and socially plausible prediction under the multimodal uncertainty that appears in real-world interaction.

Human Trajectory and Body Motion Forecasting

Embodied Intelligence & Navigation

The goal of this line of work is to enable robotic navigation where goals can be expressed at a much higher level of abstraction than traditional pipelines allow. The legacy page emphasised scene-layout prediction, active observation, and navigation that can operate in previously unseen environments.

Active Visual Navigation in an Unexplored Environment Single or Multi-UAV Planning for Discovering and Tracking Multiple Mobile Objects

JRDB: JackRabbot Dataset and Benchmark

JRDB is one of VL4AI's flagship robotic perception benchmarks and was linked from both the legacy research page and the publications/news archives. It anchors later work on pose estimation, trajectory forecasting, social understanding, and reasoning in crowded human environments.
