The LISC database contains hematological images taken from peripheral blood of healthy subjects. The database was released to enable comparative evaluation of nucleus and cytoplasm segmentation methods as well as recognition of different white blood cells in hematological images.

## Data description

Samples were taken from peripheral blood of eight normal subjects, producing 400 samples from 100 microscope slides. The slides were smeared and stained using the Gismo-Right technique and captured with a light microscope (Microscope-Axioskope 40) using an achromatic lens with 100x magnification. Images were recorded by a Sony SSCDC50AP digital camera and stored in BMP format at 720×576 resolution.

The images are colour hematology images collected from the Hematology-Oncology and BMT Research Center of Imam Khomeini Hospital in Tehran, Iran. They were classified by a hematologist into basophil, eosinophil, lymphocyte, monocyte, and neutrophil classes. Areas corresponding to nucleus and cytoplasm were manually segmented by an expert, with manual ground truth available for 250 images.

## Using the database

The database is available free of charge for research and educational use. Copying, redistribution, and unauthorised commercial use are prohibited. Use is restricted to individuals or organisations that obtained the database directly from the first author's website. Publications using the database are requested to cite:

> Rezatofighi, S.H., Soltanian-Zadeh, H.: Automatic recognition of five types of white blood cells in peripheral blood. *Computerized Medical Imaging and Graphics* 35(4) (2011) 333-343.
