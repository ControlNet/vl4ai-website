The legacy PhD call advertised **2–3 fully funded positions** for the Assured Neuro-symbolic Reasoning project.

## Focus areas preserved from the legacy page

- **Planning agent subteam:** neuro-symbolic goal recognition, neuro-symbolic path planning, neuro-symbolic reinforcement learning, and differential partial-equation modelling for dynamic systems using neural PDE solvers.
- **Multi-modal (LLM/VLM) agent subteam:** neuro-symbolic multi-modal embodied AI agents.
- **World-model subteam:** neuro-symbolic world modelling and reasoning together with formal explanation of neuro-symbolic systems.

## Desired background

The old page highlighted strong neural-network fundamentals together with experience in symbolic planning, differential equation modelling, logic reasoning (including Datalog, Problog, Linear Temporal Logic, and ASP), declarative logic programming, causality or knowledge-graph reasoning, and LLM/VLM code-generating agent modelling.

Candidates with research experience shown through a thesis or publications in top-tier ML, AI, NLP, CV, or robotics venues were described as especially competitive.

## How the legacy page asked candidates to apply

Applicants were asked to email Hamid Rezatofighi or Maria Garcia de la Banda with a CV, academic transcripts, and a key-selection-criteria response. The archived page listed **15 March 2025** as the consideration deadline.
